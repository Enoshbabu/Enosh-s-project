CONCLUSION

The Government of India's poverty line lacks consideration for inflation rates. Price fluctuations across states necessitate national adjustments for BPL families in the union budget and fiscal year. Urban-rural income disparities deepen poverty, hindering eradication. Politicians exploit poverty selectively for electoral gain, urging unified poverty metrics nationwide. Natural disasters like floods and earthquakes demand flexible BPL criteria. India must ensure BPL families access healthcare, though private hospitals neglect it. Regular practical updates on BLP criteria are crucial.
